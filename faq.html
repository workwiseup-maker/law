Common Questions
About UK Immigration

How long does a UK visa application take?

Processing times vary depending on the visa type. Work visas (Skilled Worker, Global Talent) typically take 3-8 weeks, while family visas can take 6-12 weeks. Student visas are usually processed within 3 weeks. Priority and super-priority services are available for faster processing. We recommend applying well in advance of your intended travel date and ensure all documentation is complete to avoid delays.

What is the difference between ILR and British citizenship?

Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) grants you the right to live and work in the UK permanently without time restrictions, but you remain a citizen of your home country. British citizenship (naturalisation) gives you full rights including voting, a UK passport, and the ability to live outside the UK without losing status. After holding ILR for 12 months, you can apply for British citizenship if you meet the residency and other requirements.

Can I bring my family members to the UK on my work visa?

Yes, most work visa categories allow you to bring dependent family members (spouse/partner and children under 18). Your dependents can apply at the same time as you or join you later. They will need to meet financial requirements and provide evidence of their relationship to you. Dependents on most work visas can work and study in the UK without restrictions. Each dependent requires a separate visa application and fees.

What happens if my visa application is refused?

If your application is refused, you will receive a refusal letter explaining the reasons. Depending on your circumstances, you have several options: submit a new application addressing the issues raised, request an administrative review if there was a caseworker error, or appeal the decision if you have a right of appeal. Our team can review your refusal letter, advise on the best course of action, and help strengthen a reapplication or appeal.

Do I need a sponsor licence to hire international workers?

Yes, UK employers must hold a valid sponsor licence to hire most international workers under the Skilled Worker or other sponsored visa routes. The application process involves demonstrating your business is genuine, has appropriate HR systems, and can fulfill sponsorship duties. Obtaining a licence typically takes 8 weeks. We assist businesses through the entire process, from initial application to compliance and sponsoring employees.

What are the English language requirements for UK visas?

Most UK visa routes require proof of English language proficiency, typically at CEFR Level B1 (for settlement routes) or A1 (for family visas). You can satisfy this requirement through an approved English language test (IELTS, etc.), a degree taught in English, or by being a national of a majority English-speaking country. Requirements vary by visa type, and some routes have exemptions. We can advise on which option is most suitable for your circumstances.

Visa Applications
& Requirements

Can I switch from a Student visa to a Work visa in the UK?

Yes, you can switch from a Student visa to a work visa such as Skilled Worker or Graduate visa without leaving the UK. You must apply before your current visa expires and meet all requirements for the new visa category. The Graduate visa is particularly suitable for recent graduates, allowing you to work for up to 2-3 years. For Skilled Worker, you need a job offer from a licensed sponsor meeting salary and skill level requirements.

What financial requirements must I meet for a UK visa?

Financial requirements vary significantly by visa type. Work visas generally require proof of maintenance funds (typically £1,270) held for 28 days before application, unless your sponsor certifies maintenance. Family visas have specific income thresholds (minimum £18,600 for a partner, plus additional amounts for children). Student visas require proof of course fees plus living costs. We can provide detailed guidance on the exact financial evidence needed for your specific visa category.

How much does it cost to apply for a UK visa?

Visa fees vary widely depending on the type and duration. Work visas typically range from £610-£1,500, while settlement applications cost £2,885. Most visa categories also require the Immigration Health Surcharge (£624-£1,035 per year). Priority services add £500-£1,000 to the cost. Our solicitors can provide a complete breakdown of all fees, including Home Office charges, legal fees, and additional costs for your specific application.

Can I work while my visa application is being processed?

If you apply for a new visa before your current visa expires while in the UK, you usually benefit from "Section 3C leave," which allows you to continue working under your previous visa conditions until a decision is made. However, this doesn't apply if you're switching to a visa category that doesn't allow work, or if you're applying from outside the UK. We can advise on your specific work rights during the application process.

Do I need legal representation for my visa application?

While not mandatory, professional legal representation significantly increases your chances of success, especially for complex cases. Immigration solicitors understand the detailed requirements, can identify potential issues early, ensure all documentation is correct and complete, and present your case effectively. This is particularly valuable for business visas, settlement applications, or if you've had previous refusals. Our expertise saves time, reduces stress, and helps avoid costly mistakes.

What documents do I need for a UK visa application?

Required documents vary by visa type but typically include a valid passport, biometric information, photographs, proof of English language proficiency, financial documents, and category-specific evidence (such as job offers, educational certificates, or relationship proof). Documents not in English must be professionally translated. We provide comprehensive checklists tailored to your specific visa application and review all documents before submission to ensure compliance with Home Office requirements.
