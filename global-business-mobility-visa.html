Global Business Mobility (GBM) Visa

The Global Business Mobility (GBM) visa routes enable multinational businesses to transfer employees, including senior managers, specialist workers, graduate trainees, and those establishing a UK presence, from their overseas operations to UK branches or subsidiaries. These visa categories facilitate global mobility for businesses with international operations while ensuring that workers meet skill and salary requirements and that their assignments in the UK are genuine and temporary.

Our immigration solicitors provide comprehensive guidance to multinational organizations and their employees on navigating the various GBM visa routes. We assist with determining the most appropriate GBM category for each assignment, obtaining the necessary sponsor licences, and ensuring compliance with all Home Office requirements throughout the duration of employees' UK assignments.

Senior or Specialist Worker (GBM) Visa

The Senior or Specialist Worker route is for established employees of multinational companies who are being transferred to a UK entity as senior managers or specialist employees with skills, knowledge, or experience essential to the UK operation. To qualify, you must have worked for the same employer or group of companies outside the UK for at least 12 months immediately before your transfer, and your role in the UK must be at RQF Level 6 or above (graduate level) with a minimum salary threshold.

This visa is initially granted for up to five years (or up to nine years for high earners), and while it does not directly lead to settlement, senior or specialist workers may switch to other visa categories that do offer a settlement pathway. The sponsoring UK entity must hold a valid GBM sponsor licence and demonstrate that the transfer is necessary for the UK operation. Our team assists employers with sponsor licence applications and guides employees through the visa application process, ensuring all documentation evidences the qualifying employment relationship and the specialized nature of the role.

UK Expansion Worker (GBM) Visa

The UK Expansion Worker route is designed for senior managers or specialist employees being transferred to the UK to establish a branch or subsidiary of an overseas business that does not yet have a UK presence. This category allows overseas businesses to send key personnel to set up operations in the UK, provided the worker has been employed by the overseas business for at least 12 months and will undertake senior management or specialist duties essential to establishing the UK entity.

UK Expansion Worker visas are granted for up to two years and cannot be extended beyond this period. During this time, the worker must actively work toward establishing the UK operation, and the UK entity must obtain its own sponsor licence before the initial two-year period expires if it wishes to continue employing the worker or sponsor others. This route provides a pathway for international businesses to enter the UK market using trusted senior personnel. Our solicitors advise on the UK expansion strategy, assist with sponsor licence applications for the overseas entity, and provide ongoing support to ensure the UK operation is established in compliance with immigration and corporate requirements.

Graduate Trainee (GBM) Visa

The Graduate Trainee route allows multinational companies to transfer recent graduates to the UK for training and work experience as part of a structured graduate training program. To qualify, you must have been employed by the overseas entity for at least three months, hold a relevant degree-level qualification obtained within the three years preceding your assignment, and be participating in a clearly defined graduate training program with progression milestones and learning objectives.

Graduate Trainee visas are granted for up to twelve months (or up to two years for graduate trainees in high-demand occupations such as healthcare). This route provides recent graduates within multinational organizations the opportunity to gain UK work experience while developing their skills through structured training. Salary requirements are generally lower than other GBM routes, recognizing the training nature of the assignments. Our immigration team helps multinational employers structure compliant graduate training programs, obtain GBM sponsor licences, and sponsor graduate trainees, ensuring programs meet Home Office criteria while supporting talent development across international operations.

Secondment Worker (GBM) Visa

The Secondment Worker route facilitates the temporary transfer of employees from an overseas business to a linked UK organization for specific work assignments or projects. This category requires that you have been employed by the overseas entity for at least 12 months and are being seconded under a contract for services or to fulfill a contract between the UK entity and your employer. Your role must be at an appropriate skill level, and you must meet minimum salary thresholds.

Secondment Worker visas can be granted for up to five years (or up to nine years in total), and extensions are possible as long as the secondment arrangement continues to meet the requirements. This route is particularly useful for service contracts, joint ventures, and collaboration agreements between related entities across jurisdictions. The UK entity must hold a GBM sponsor licence and demonstrate the genuine need for the secondment. Our solicitors advise on structuring secondment arrangements to meet immigration requirements, assist with sponsor licensing, and ensure compliance throughout the secondment period.

Service Supplier (GBM) Visa

The Service Supplier route is for employees of overseas companies who are coming to the UK to provide services under a contract between their employer and a UK-based client, typically as part of international trade agreements. To qualify, you must be employed by the overseas service provider, be assigned to supply services to a UK client under a qualifying contract, and meet the skill and salary requirements for your role. This route allows businesses to fulfill service contracts in the UK using their own staff rather than recruiting UK-based personnel.

Service Supplier visas are generally granted for up to twelve months at a time, with a maximum cumulative stay. The contract between the overseas employer and UK client must be genuine, documented, and for services that require the physical presence of the worker in the UK. This route is particularly relevant for businesses providing professional services, IT support, consultancy, and technical services to UK clients. Our immigration team assists overseas service providers and their UK clients with understanding eligibility, structuring service contracts to meet immigration requirements, obtaining sponsor licences, and sponsoring service suppliers, ensuring smooth delivery of contracted services while maintaining full immigration compliance.
