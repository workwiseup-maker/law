Self Sponsorship Visa

Self-sponsorship is an innovative immigration strategy that allows foreign nationals to establish their own UK company, obtain a sponsor licence for that company, and then sponsor themselves for a Skilled Worker Visa. This route provides entrepreneurs and skilled professionals with the flexibility to work for their own UK business while maintaining the immigration benefits of the Skilled Worker route, including the pathway to settlement.

Our immigration solicitors provide end-to-end guidance on the self-sponsorship process, from company formation and sponsor licence applications to individual visa applications and ongoing compliance. We help you navigate the regulatory requirements and ensure your self-sponsorship arrangement is structured properly to satisfy Home Office scrutiny while supporting your business objectives.

What is Self Sponsorship?

Self-sponsorship involves establishing a UK company (typically a limited company), applying for a sponsor licence on behalf of that company, and then using that licence to sponsor yourself for a Skilled Worker Visa. While the concept is legally permissible, the Home Office scrutinizes self-sponsorship applications carefully to ensure they are genuine and comply with all immigration and employment laws. The sponsoring company must be a real, operating business with genuine staffing needs, not merely a vehicle created solely for immigration purposes.

This route is particularly suitable for entrepreneurs who want to establish and grow their own businesses in the UK, senior executives transferring their international business operations to the UK, or skilled professionals who wish to provide services through their own UK company. Self-sponsorship offers greater control and flexibility than traditional employment arrangements, while still providing a clear pathway to permanent residence. Our solicitors assess your circumstances to determine whether self-sponsorship is the right strategy and guide you through structuring your arrangement to meet both immigration requirements and business goals.

Setting Up a UK Company

The first step in self-sponsorship is establishing a legitimate UK company, usually a private limited company registered with Companies House. Your company must have a genuine business purpose, maintain proper accounts, comply with UK tax obligations, and demonstrate the capacity to fulfill sponsor duties. The company structure should include appropriate shareholding and directorship arrangements, and you must show that the company has sufficient financial resources to meet its obligations to you as a sponsored employee.

Key considerations include drafting a comprehensive business plan, ensuring adequate capitalization, establishing proper HR and payroll systems, and maintaining professional premises or a credible trading address. The Home Office will examine whether your company is genuinely trading or capable of trading, whether it has the necessary systems and personnel to comply with sponsorship duties, and whether your role within the company is at the appropriate skill and salary level. Our team assists with all aspects of company formation and structuring, ensuring your setup meets immigration compliance standards while providing a solid foundation for business growth.

Route to Permanent Residency

Once successfully self-sponsored on a Skilled Worker Visa, you can work toward settlement (indefinite leave to remain) after five years of continuous residence in the UK. Throughout this period, you must maintain your sponsor licence, continue to be employed by your sponsoring company, meet the minimum salary requirements, and comply with all sponsorship and immigration conditions. Your company must remain operational and continue to satisfy the Home Office that it is a genuine business with legitimate reasons for sponsoring you.

The pathway to settlement through self-sponsorship is identical to other Skilled Worker routes, provided all requirements are maintained. After obtaining indefinite leave to remain, you may apply for British citizenship following an additional twelve months of residence. Our solicitors provide ongoing compliance support throughout your journey, including assistance with sponsor licence renewals, visa extensions, addressing Home Office queries, and ultimately guiding you through settlement and citizenship applications. We ensure your self-sponsorship arrangement remains compliant and positions you for long-term success in the UK.
