Sponsor Licence Applications

A UK sponsor licence is a legal requirement for organizations wishing to employ workers from outside the UK under the Skilled Worker, Health and Care Worker, Global Business Mobility, Scale-up, or other sponsored work routes. Obtaining and maintaining a sponsor licence involves demonstrating to the Home Office that your organization is genuine, capable of fulfilling its sponsorship duties, and compliant with UK immigration laws. The sponsor licence system ensures that only responsible employers can sponsor overseas workers.

Our immigration solicitors provide comprehensive support to UK businesses throughout the sponsor licence lifecycle, from initial applications and compliance audits to renewals, suspension appeals, and revocation challenges. We help organizations understand their sponsorship obligations, implement compliant HR systems, and maintain the high standards required by the Home Office to protect and preserve their sponsor licence status.

Skilled Worker Sponsor Licence

The Skilled Worker Sponsor Licence allows UK employers to recruit skilled workers from overseas to fill vacancies at RQF Level 3 or above (equivalent to A-level). To obtain this licence, your organization must be operating lawfully in the UK, have appropriate HR systems and personnel to manage sponsorship duties, demonstrate genuine vacancies requiring overseas recruitment, and show that you can comply with record-keeping, monitoring, and reporting requirements. The application process requires extensive documentation including business registration, insurance, HR policies, and premises evidence.

Once granted, Skilled Worker sponsors must maintain continuous compliance with sponsorship duties including conducting right-to-work checks, keeping detailed records, reporting changes in sponsored workers' circumstances, and cooperating with Home Office compliance visits. Our team assists with preparing sponsor licence applications, ensuring your HR infrastructure meets Home Office standards, and providing ongoing compliance support to maintain your licence in good standing. A Skilled Worker Sponsor Licence is the foundation for building an international workforce and accessing global talent to support business growth.

Temporary Worker Sponsor Licence

The Temporary Worker Sponsor Licence enables UK organizations to sponsor overseas workers for short-term assignments under various categories including creative workers, charity workers, religious workers, seasonal workers, and international agreement workers. Unlike the Skilled Worker route, Temporary Worker visas are designed for genuinely temporary roles that do not lead to settlement. Requirements for obtaining a Temporary Worker licence are similar to Skilled Worker licences, including demonstrating organizational legitimacy, HR capacity, and compliance systems.

Temporary Worker sponsors must ensure that roles are genuinely temporary, workers do not exceed the permitted duration for their specific category, and all sponsorship duties are fulfilled throughout the workers' assignments. Our solicitors advise on which Temporary Worker category is appropriate for your needs, assist with licence applications, and provide guidance on managing temporary worker sponsorships, ensuring your organization benefits from short-term international talent while maintaining full compliance with immigration regulations.

Global Business Mobility Sponsor Licence

The Global Business Mobility (GBM) Sponsor Licence allows multinational businesses to transfer employees from overseas branches to UK operations under various GBM routes including Senior or Specialist Worker, UK Expansion Worker, Graduate Trainee, Secondment Worker, and Service Supplier categories. To qualify for a GBM licence, your organization must be part of a multinational business with operations in multiple countries, demonstrate the need for intra-company transfers, and meet all standard sponsor licence requirements regarding HR systems, compliance capacity, and organizational legitimacy.

GBM sponsors face additional requirements specific to multinational transfers, including evidencing group structure, overseas employment relationships, and the necessity of transfers to UK operations. Our immigration team assists multinational organizations with obtaining GBM sponsor licences, structuring compliant transfer arrangements, and managing sponsorship obligations across international operations. We help ensure your global mobility programs operate smoothly while satisfying UK immigration requirements and supporting your business's international talent deployment strategies.

Scale Up Worker Sponsor Licence

The Scale-up Worker Sponsor Licence is designed for high-growth UK businesses that meet specific growth criteria and wish to sponsor skilled workers under the Scale-up Visa route. To qualify as a scale-up sponsor, your organization must demonstrate annualized employment or revenue growth of at least 20% over a three-year period, starting with a minimum of 10 employees. In addition to growth metrics, you must meet all standard sponsor licence requirements and show capacity to fulfill sponsorship duties despite your organization's rapid expansion phase.

Scale-up sponsors benefit from attracting talented workers who gain flexibility after an initial six-month sponsored period, though sponsors must still maintain their licence and fulfill sponsorship duties during this initial period. Our solicitors assist scale-up businesses with verifying growth criteria, preparing sponsor licence applications that demonstrate both growth and compliance capacity, and ensuring ongoing adherence to sponsorship obligations. We help fast-growing businesses leverage the Scale-up route to access international talent while managing the regulatory requirements that come with sponsor licence status.

Sponsor Licence Compliance Support

Maintaining sponsor licence compliance is an ongoing obligation that requires continuous attention to Home Office requirements. Sponsors must keep detailed records of sponsored workers, conduct and document right-to-work checks, report changes in circumstances within required timeframes, respond to Home Office queries promptly, and cooperate with compliance visits. Failure to meet these obligations can result in licence suspension or revocation, action against individual sponsored workers, or civil penalties. Effective compliance requires robust HR systems, trained personnel, and proactive management of sponsorship duties.

Our immigration team provides comprehensive compliance support including compliance audits, HR systems reviews, training for Authorising Officers and Key Contacts, guidance on reporting obligations, and assistance with responding to Home Office compliance inquiries. We help sponsors implement best practices for record-keeping, develop internal compliance procedures, and maintain the high standards required by the Home Office. Regular compliance reviews and proactive management significantly reduce the risk of licence issues and ensure your organization can continue to benefit from sponsoring international workers without interruption.

Sponsor Licence Renewal

Sponsor licences are granted for four years, after which they must be renewed to continue sponsoring workers. The renewal process requires demonstrating that your organization continues to meet all sponsor licence requirements, has maintained compliance with sponsorship duties during the licence period, and retains the capacity and systems to fulfill ongoing obligations. The Home Office scrutinizes renewal applications carefully, reviewing compliance history, any changes to organizational structure, and current HR systems and processes.

Organizations should begin preparing for renewal several months before their licence expires to ensure continuity of sponsorship authority. Our solicitors assist with sponsor licence renewals by conducting pre-renewal compliance audits, preparing renewal applications, gathering updated documentation, and addressing any compliance concerns that may affect renewal prospects. We help ensure your renewal application demonstrates ongoing compliance and organizational capability, minimizing the risk of delays or refusal and ensuring uninterrupted ability to sponsor international workers to support your business operations.

Sponsor Licence Suspension

The Home Office may suspend a sponsor licence when concerns arise about compliance, organizational changes, or other issues that require investigation before deciding whether to revoke the licence permanently. During suspension, sponsors cannot assign new Certificates of Sponsorship or vary existing sponsorships, though current sponsored workers may generally continue their employment. Common reasons for suspension include compliance failures, failure to respond to Home Office queries, suspected abuse of the immigration system, or significant organizational changes that affect sponsorship capacity.

When a licence is suspended, sponsors typically have 20 working days to provide evidence addressing the Home Office's concerns and demonstrating why the licence should be reinstated rather than revoked. Our immigration team provides urgent assistance with licence suspensions, helping you understand the reasons for suspension, gather responsive evidence, prepare detailed representations, and engage with the Home Office to resolve concerns. Swift, comprehensive responses to suspension notices are critical to protecting your sponsor licence and your ability to continue employing international workers.

Sponsor Licence Revocation

Sponsor licence revocation is the most serious action the Home Office can take against a sponsor, permanently removing the organization's ability to sponsor workers. Revocation typically follows serious compliance failures, deliberate breaches of sponsorship duties, providing false information, or failing to address concerns raised during suspension. When a licence is revoked, existing Certificates of Sponsorship are cancelled, sponsored workers may face curtailment of their visas, and the organization is barred from re-applying for a sponsor licence for a specified period (often 12 months).

Organizations facing revocation have limited options but may be able to appeal or request administrative review depending on the circumstances. The impact of revocation extends beyond immigration, potentially affecting business operations, international contracts, and recruitment strategies. Our solicitors provide representation for organizations facing licence revocation, challenging revocation decisions where grounds exist, advising on protecting affected employees, and ultimately assisting with re-application once the cooling-off period expires. We help organizations understand what went wrong, implement corrective measures, and rebuild compliance frameworks to support successful future sponsor licence applications.

Certificates of Sponsorship

A Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) is an electronic record, not a physical document, that UK sponsors assign to individual overseas workers as a prerequisite for their visa applications. Each CoS contains detailed information about the sponsored worker, their role, salary, occupation code, and sponsorship circumstances. Sponsors must apply for and obtain CoS allocations from the Home Office, with different processes for defined (requiring advance allocation) and undefined (available on demand for certain categories) Certificates of Sponsorship.

Assigning a CoS is a serious responsibility requiring sponsors to verify that the information is accurate, the role is genuine and meets eligibility criteria, and the worker is suitable for sponsorship. Mistakes or misrepresentations in CoS assignments can result in compliance action against the sponsor. Our immigration team advises sponsors on CoS allocation strategies, guides them through the assignment process, ensures CoS information is accurate and compliant, and helps manage CoS allocations to support organizational recruitment needs. Proper CoS management is essential to effective sponsorship and maintaining good standing with the Home Office throughout your sponsor licence period.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I immigrate to the UK, and how can Bekenbey Solicitors assist me?

There are many visa categories for immigrating to the UK. As Bekenbey Solicitors, we determine the most suitable visa type for your situation and professionally manage your application process. Our solicitors guide you on matters such as family reunion, work permits, or innovator visas.

I'm planning to travel to the UK; how can I find out which visa type I should apply for?

Depending on your purpose of travel, a Standard Visitor visa or other visitor visas may be suitable. You can contact our office for a free preliminary assessment to determine the most appropriate visa for you.

How do I apply for a UK Work Visa (e.g., Skilled Worker visa)?

Work visa applications require a Certificate of Sponsorship from a licensed UK employer, an employment contract, and evidence of qualifications and experience. Our solicitors will inform you in detail about the necessary documents and provide support throughout the process.

Will there be an interview during the immigration process?

Yes, an interview is conducted at the Visa Application Centre for most visa applications. Our solicitors provide detailed preparation support before the interview to ease the process for you.

My UK visa application was refused, what should I do?

If your visa is refused, it is essential to consult an immigration solicitor immediately. We can analyze the refusal reasons and create a strategy for a re-application or appeal, where applicable.

How long does the UK visa application process take?

Visa application processing times vary depending on the type of visa. While some visas are processed in a few weeks, others may take several months. We will inform you in detail about the estimated duration of your application process.
